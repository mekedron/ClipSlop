# Your first transformation

ClipSlop is installed, permissions are granted, a provider is configured. Time for the moment of truth.

1. Open any app — Notes, Mail, Slack, your editor.
2. Type or paste a sentence with a deliberate typo: *"i recieved your email yesterday and i wanted too say thanks for there quick reply."*
3. Select the sentence.
4. Press <kbd>⌃</kbd><kbd>⌘</kbd><kbd>G</kbd>. ClipSlop runs **Fix Grammar** in the background.
5. The corrected text replaces the selection.

That's **Quick Paste** — the fastest mode. No app switch, no dialog, no copy-paste dance.

<Callout type="tip" title="What just happened?">
ClipSlop simulated <kbd>⌘C</kbd> to read your selection, ran the **Fix Grammar** prompt against the configured provider, and simulated <kbd>⌘V</kbd> with the result. The clipboard is restored to whatever was on it before the run.
</Callout>

## Want more than one step?

Press <kbd>⌃</kbd><kbd>⌘</kbd><kbd>C</kbd> instead. ClipSlop opens its floating panel with the selection, and you can chain prompts together: translate, then rewrite, then format. See [The full pipeline](./use/full-pipeline.mdx).
